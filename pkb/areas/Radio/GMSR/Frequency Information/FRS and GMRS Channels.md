**FRS/GMRS Channels:** In the United States, the Family Radio Service (FRS) and the General Mobile Radio Service (GMRS) are two types of services that allow for license-free or license-by-rule (for GMRS) operations, respectively, on specific frequencies. 

### FRS Channels (License-Free)

FRS is license-free and intended for personal or family use. As of my last update, there are 22 FRS channels, which are:

- **Channels 1-7 and 15-22** can be used for FRS and have a power limit of 2 watts.
- **Channels 8-14** are limited to 0.5 watts and are exclusively FRS.

### GMRS Channels (License Required)

GMRS requires a license in the U.S. but does not require an individual to pass an examination. Family members of the licensee can also operate GMRS stations within the license’s terms. GMRS shares some frequencies with FRS but allows for higher power limits and the use of repeaters.

- **Channels 1-7 and 15-22** can be used for GMRS. The power limit for GMRS on these shared channels is up to 50 watts.
- **GMRS-Only Channels:** There are additional channels specifically allocated for GMRS, often used with repeaters, which are not shared with FRS.
