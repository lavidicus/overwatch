# Completing Plex Configuration

After claiming and setting up the Plex container, it is important to ensure that it is secure. Here are the steps to follow:

## 1. Set up SSL/TLS encryption

To secure your Plex container, you need to set up SSL/TLS encryption. This can be achieved by obtaining an SSL/TLS certificate from a trusted certificate authority and configuring your Plex container to use it. You can also use Let's Encrypt to obtain a free SSL/TLS certificate.

## 2. Enable authentication

You should enable authentication for your Plex container to prevent unauthorized access. This can be achieved by creating user accounts and passwords. You can also use a third-party authentication provider such as Google or Facebook.

## 3. Use a VPN

Using a VPN can help secure your Plex container by encrypting your internet connection and preventing eavesdropping. It also allows you to access your Plex container remotely without exposing it to the public internet.

## 4. Configure firewall rules

Configure firewall rules to restrict access to your Plex container. You should only allow access from trusted networks and block all other traffic.

By default, Plex uses port 32400 for its web interface and media server. However, additional ports may be required depending on your configuration, such as for remote access or for specific media types. It is recommended to consult the Plex documentation for specific port requirements based on your use case.

## 5. Keep your Plex container up to date

Make sure to keep your Plex container up to date by installing the latest security patches and updates. This will help ensure that your Plex container remains secure.

By following these steps, you can make sure that your Plex container is secure and protected from unauthorized access.
